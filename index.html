Ethnographic Analysis: Rules of the Code

Linguistic Adaptation

With experienced players, I use specialized gaming terminology ("edge-guarding", "recoveries", "sairs") that indexes membership in the gaming community. With novices, I switch to simple, comprehensible, & less precise language.

Bodily Performance

With friend I have competitive, invested body tension & posture (forward lean, facing screen). With sibling, I am relaxed & oriented towards sibling to help (turned body, pointing gestures). Different body states (relaxed, orientation) indicate roles of participant vs. instructor.

Proxemic Rules

Distance: 4 feet (teammate distance) -> 6 feet (non-participant distance). Greater distance = less distractions for sibling (ADHD).

Social Indexicality

Code switches index our social frameworks: expertise of peer-with-peer vs. advising of expert-with-novice. Code-switching adapts to the conversation partner's needs (focused planning w/ friend, non-distracting assistance w/ sibling).

Ethnographic Analysis: Rules of the Code

Institutional Background

Professional LA Fitness code: artifically upbeat tone & institutional vocabulary ("membership", "registration", "studio") to index my job as a service worker. My peer code: informal language, indexes casual workplace 'friendship'.

Kinesic Signaling

Body: Upright, professional posture -> lax, unprofessional culture. With members: formal gestures (polite waves, pointing to resources). With colleagues: casual gestures (eye rolls, dismissive waves) to build camaraderie.

Proxemic Boundaries

Distance: professional (3 feet) -> personal (1.5 feet), marking a boundary between customer service & colleague socialization.

Social Function

Maintains professional boundaries w/ customers & builds camaraderie w/ colleagues. Switches index different relations: service transaction vs peer bonding.

Ethnographic Analysis: Rules of the Code

Prosodic Adaptation

Vocal switch: high-pitched, variable intonation (affectionate) -> low, monotone delivery (disciplinary). This is because, for dogs, pitch variation = positive & monotone = negative.

Kinesic Reinforcement

Body posture: petting & crouches (same level, non-threatening) -> standing tensely (height intimidating dog). Facial expressions: exaggerated(?) positivity -> neutral seriousness. Visual cues to reinforce tones' messages.

Linguistic Simplicity

Both registers use simple, repetitive language. Positive register uses questions + exclamations; disciplinary register uses seriousness + commands.

Interspecies Social Rules

This indexes our understanding of canid communication and our relationship as owners vs pets. Switches establish boundaries (disciplinary) & bonding (affectionate).
