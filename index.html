Brawlhalla Player: Advanced vs Simple Language

üéÆ
With Players
üë¶
With Sibling
Teammate:

"Their Rayman keeps spamming dair approaches. How should we counter?"

You:

"We need to coordinate our edge-guarding and punish their recovery options. Let's space with nairs and look for a gimp."

Sibling:

"How do I beat this guy? He keeps jumping at me!"

You:

"Just hit them when they try to come back to the platform. Watch out when he jumps at you from above."

What's happening here?

When talking to experienced players, you use advanced gaming terminology ("edge-guarding", "recovery options", "nairs", "gimp"). With your sibling, you simplify the language to basic concepts they can understand.

üôã
With Member
üë•
With Colleague
Member:

"Hi, I'm interested in joining a yoga class. What's available this week?"

You:

"Good morning! Our new yoga class starts at 6 PM. Would you like me to check availability for you?"

Colleague:

"Another membership question? The schedule's right there on the wall!"

You:

"I know, right? Third time this week! At least it's an easy one to answer."

What's happening here?

With members, you use formal, helpful language appropriate for customer service. With colleagues, you switch to informal, conversational language where you can express frustration or share inside perspectives.
